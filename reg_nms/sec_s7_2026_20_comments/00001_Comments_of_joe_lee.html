Subject: Comment on File No. [S7-2026-20] — Proposed Amendments to Regulation NMS To the Securities and Exchange Commission: I am a retail investor and I oppose the proposed rescission of Rule 611 of Regulation NMS and the removal of the prohibition on locked and crossed quotations. My concern is that this proposal could weaken protections that help ordinary investors receive fair executions. Retail investors generally do not control order routing, venue selection, wholesaler arrangements, or execution algorithms. We depend on brokers, best execution obligations, and clear market-wide price protection rules. Removing Rule 611 may give sophisticated market participants more flexibility, but I do not see how it benefits ordinary retail investors. It could make execution quality harder to evaluate, reduce the usefulness of the NBBO as a benchmark, and increase the risk that different brokers provide materially different fills for similar orders. If the Commission proceeds, it should not remove these protections unless it first adopts stronger retail execution-quality safeguards, clearer broker routing disclosures, and better tools for ordinary investors to compare actual execution quality across brokers. I respectfully urge the Commission to retain Rule 611 and the locked/crossed quotation protections, or at minimum delay any repeal until the Commission can show with evidence that retail investors will not be harmed. Respectfully, Joe Lee Retail Investor
