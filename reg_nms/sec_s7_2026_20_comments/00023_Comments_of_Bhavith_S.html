Dear SEC Commissioners,

As a retail investor, I am writing to strongly oppose the proposal to rescind the Order Protection Rule (Rule 611) under Regulation NMS.

Currently, I already notice discrepancies between the displayed prices of assets and the actual prices at which my trades are executed. In unregulated markets like cryptocurrency, this type of slippage is common. I am deeply concerned that repealing Rule 611 will bring that same lack of strict price protection to the U.S. stock market, exacerbating the problem for everyday traders.

My primary concern is the inherent conflict of interest at zero-commission brokerages like Robinhood and Webull. If the strict mandate to route orders to the venue displaying the National Best Bid and Offer (NBBO) is removed, these brokerages will be highly incentivized to route retail orders to favored off-exchange wholesalers and alternative venues that pay them the most in Payment for Order Flow (PFOF).

Without the rigid mechanical protection of the Trade-Through Rule, brokerages will prioritize their own kickbacks and revenue over securing the absolute best execution price for their customers. The subjective "best execution" standard is not a sufficient replacement for a hard, mathematical rule that guarantees retail investors get the best publicly displayed price.

Please do not remove the very safeguard that ensures retail trades are not traded-through for the benefit of corporate middlemen. Keep Rule 611 in place.

Thank you for your time and for protecting retail investors.
